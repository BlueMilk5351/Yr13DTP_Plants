About New Zealand

Climate

New Zealand is in the southern hemisphere in the Pacific Ocean. It is a predominantly temperate oceanic climate with average annual temperatures ranging from 10°C in the south to 16°C in the north. Conditions vary sharply across regions from extremely wet on the West Coast of the South Island to semi-arid in Central Otago and the Mackenzie Basin of inland Canterbury and subtropical in Northland.

Regions

New Zealand is divided into sixteen regions, eleven of which are administered by regional councils and five by unitary authorities. Chatham Islands is classed as a territory and not a region and is under its own legislation. The largest region is Canterbury and it has an area of 44,504 km2 and the smallest region is Nelson with 422km2 .
